## Probability

Probability abstractly is a relationship between two sets. Commonly, it is the set of desired outcomes divided by the set of total 
outcomes. For example, the probability that if you roll a $7$ on two six sided dice can be thought of as the number of ways to roll
a $7$ divided by the total number of possible ways to roll two dice.

We refer to the set of all outcomes as the **sample space** commonly denoted $\Omega$. A subset of the sample space is called an 
**event**. In our example above, we looked at the event where a seven was rolled, but we could image many different events in the same 
sample space of 36 possible outcomes.
